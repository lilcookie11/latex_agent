\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}






In this section, we conduct extensive experiments to verify the effectiveness of \M~by addressing the following research questions: 

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]

\item \textbf{RQ1:} How does the performance of \M~compare to that of baseline methods in composed image retrieval?


\item \textbf{RQ2:} How do different components of our \M~contribute to the overall performance?

\item \textbf{RQ3:} How does the model performance vary to the key hyperparameters of \M?

\item \textbf{RQ4:} How does multi-agent collaboration enhance the performance of our \M~in the task of composed image retrieval?


\end{itemize} 

\subsection{Experimental Settings}\label{sec:settings}

A concise summary of the experimental settings is provided below, while additional details on the datasets, baselines, and model implementation can be found.

\noindent - \textit{Datasets}. To validate the effectiveness of our \M, we conduct experiments on five datasets that incorporate both synthetic and real-world domain shifts. For synthetic shifts, we utilize two widely used multimodal datasets: Kinetics50 \cite{kay2017kinetics} and VGGSound \cite{chen2020vggsound}. Following established protocols~\cite{yang2024test,Lee2024Entropy}, we introduce 15 types of video corruptions and 6 types of audio corruptions into these datasets to simulate distribution shifts commonly encountered in real-world scenarios. Each corruption type is applied at 5 severity levels. For real-world domain shifts, we evaluate on three real-world datasets: CMU-MOSI~\cite{zadeh2016multimodal}, CMU-MOSEI~\cite{zadeh2018multimodal}, and CH-SIMS~\cite{yu2020ch}, each comprising three modalities (e.g., text, audio, and video).


\noindent - \textit{Baselines}. To comprehensively evaluate the test-time adaptation capability of our \M, we compare it against 13 competitive baselines, which can be broadly categorized into two groups: (1) \textit{TTA-based methods}: Tent \cite{Wang2021Tent}, SAR \cite{Niu2023Stable}, READ \cite{yang2024test}, DeYO \cite{Lee2024Entropy}, SuMi \cite{Guo2025Smoothing}, and PTA \cite{wangpartition}; (2) \textit{CTTA-based methods}: CoTTA \cite{wang2022continual}, FOA \cite{Niu2024FOA}, ViDA \cite{Liu2024ViDA}, CMAE \cite{liu2024continual}, BECoTTA \cite{Lee2024BECoTTA}, REM \cite{Han2025Ranked}, and PALM \cite{maharana2025palm}.

\noindent - \textit{Metrics}. Following prior works~\cite{yang2024test, Guo2025Smoothing}, we use accuracy as the primary evaluation metric for all datasets, and additionally report the F1 score for CMU-MOSI, CMU-MOSEI, and CH-SIMS.
