\section{Introduction}

% 中文：多模态持续测试时适配旨在通过在推理阶段利用无标签目标域测试流进行在线适配，提升多模态模型在持续变化分布偏移下的泛化能力。
% 该任务对于真实部署尤为重要，因为测试样本通常是顺序到达的，且目标分布会动态变化。

% 尽管这些方法有效，基于反向传播的适配需要保存中间激活并执行梯度计算，从而带来显著的计算、显存和时延开销；
% 在资源受限的端侧部署中，尤其是对于全精度或压缩不足的模型，这类开销会限制其实用性。
% 因此，本文研究一个更严格但更贴近实际部署的设定：在资源受限、仅支持前向传播、单样本流式输入的端侧环境中，实现稳定而有效的多模态持续测试时适配。
Multimodal continual test-time adaptation (CTTA) aims to improve model generalization under continuously evolving distribution shifts by adapting multimodal models to unlabeled target-domain streams during inference. This is critical for real-world deployment, where test samples arrive sequentially and target distributions often change dynamically. Most existing CTTA methods rely on backpropagation (BP) to update model parameters through auxiliary objectives such as entropy minimization or self-supervision. While effective, BP-based adaptation incurs substantial computational, memory, and latency overhead due to activation storage and gradient computation, which limits its practicality in resource-constrained edge-side deployment, especially for full-precision or insufficiently compressed models. Therefore, this work studies a more stringent yet practically relevant setting: stable and effective multimodal continual test-time adaptation under resource-constrained, forward-only, single-sample streaming edge deployment.

% 然而，这些方法通常仍依赖反向传播来更新模型参数，难以直接适用于资源受限的端侧部署，尤其是在低算力、低存储或包含量化组件的设备上。
% 为绕开反向传播带来的计算和显存开销，已有 BP-free TTA 方法开始探索仅依赖轻量统计或前向计算的在线适配策略。
% 例如，BN-Adapt 通过调整 BatchNorm 统计量实现快速适配，T3A 通过更新分类器原型进行测试时调整，FOA 则利用进化搜索优化 Vision Transformer 中的轻量 prompt。
% 总体而言，现有 BP-free TTA 方法仍难以直接扩展到端侧多模态 CTTA，主要受限于两点：
% \textbf{1）架构泛化性不足。} BN-Adapt 依赖带有 BatchNorm 的 CNN，FOA 依赖 ViT prompt 结构，二者都难以自然覆盖异质的模态分支与融合模块。
% \textbf{2）优化容量有限。} 现有方法主要更新统计量、分类器原型或 prompt 等低维组件，难以有效吸收复杂 branch 表征内部的模态偏移。
Existing multimodal TTA studies mainly focus on diagnosing shifted, biased, or unreliable modalities, and mitigate cross-modal distribution shifts through modality reliability modeling, modality selection, asymmetric adaptation, or supervision correction. However, these methods typically still rely on BP to update model parameters, making them difficult to deploy directly in resource-constrained edge-side environments. To bypass the computational and memory overhead of backpropagation, existing BP-free TTA methods have explored online adaptation using only lightweight statistics or forward computations. BN-Adapt performs rapid adaptation by adjusting BatchNorm statistics, T3A updates classifier prototypes at test time, and FOA optimizes lightweight prompts for Vision Transformers (ViTs) via evolutionary search. Overall, existing BP-free TTA methods remain difficult to directly extend to edge-side multimodal CTTA, due to two fundamental limitations: \textbf{1) Poor architectural generality.} BN-Adapt relies on CNNs with BatchNorm, while FOA depends on ViT prompt structures; neither naturally covers heterogeneous modality branches and fusion modules. \textbf{2) Limited optimization capacity.} Existing methods mainly update low-dimensional statistics, classifier prototypes, or prompts, making them insufficient for absorbing modality-specific shifts inside complex branch representations.

% 同时，它不依赖特定的模型结构，因而具有更强的适用性。
% 然而，在端侧单样本流式环境中，直接将 ZOO 应用于在线无监督多模态 TTA 在实践中效果仍然不好。
% 我们的诊断分析表明，这主要源于两个关键挑战：
% \textbf{1）样本空间与参数空间的双重不可靠。} 在单样本流式设置下，ZOO 的更新方向容易受到不可靠样本干扰，从而将模型牵引到错误方向；
% 同时，多模态模型高维且异质的参数空间会使 ZOO 收敛缓慢、效率低下，并难以准确作用于真正需要吸收偏移的关键参数。
% \textbf{2）不稳定的优化目标。} 在 ZOO 通过前向扰动估计更新方向的情形下，熵损失随样本和分布变化产生的剧烈波动会直接转化为高方差的方向估计，从而导致在线更新不稳定。
Zeroth-order optimization (ZOO) offers a natural path toward bypassing the above limitations. As a theoretically grounded BP-free learning mechanism, ZOO estimates update directions through forward-pass perturbations and requires no backward computation, making it more suitable for resource-constrained edge-side deployment. Meanwhile, it does not rely on specific model structures, and thus provides stronger architectural applicability. However, under edge-side single-sample streaming deployment, directly applying ZOO to online unsupervised multimodal TTA remains inferior in practice. Our diagnostic analysis reveals two key challenges: \textbf{1) Dual unreliability in sample and parameter spaces.} In single-sample streaming adaptation, ZOO update directions are easily disturbed by unreliable samples, which can drive the model toward incorrect adaptation trajectories. Meanwhile, the high-dimensional and heterogeneous parameter space of multimodal models makes ZOO slow to converge and inefficient to search, preventing it from accurately targeting the key parameters that truly need shift absorption. \textbf{2) Unstable optimization objective.} When ZOO estimates update directions through forward perturbations, heavy fluctuations in entropy loss caused by sample and distribution changes are directly translated into high-variance direction estimates, resulting in unstable online updates.

To address these challenges, we propose
\textbf{B}ranch-\textbf{F}irst \textbf{C}onservative \textbf{A}daptation \textbf{(BFCA)},
a stable forward-only framework for edge-side multimodal continual test-time adaptation.
BFCA does not naively apply ZOO to all test samples and all model parameters; instead, it stabilizes ZOO from two complementary aspects: the search space and the optimization objective.
(1) \textbf{Reliable Sample and Layer Selection (RSLS).}
To handle the dual unreliability in sample and parameter spaces, RSLS reduces the interference of noisy samples through reliable sample screening, while constraining ZOO to selected branch layers that are more likely to absorb modality-specific shifts.
This avoids inefficient search over the full high-dimensional parameter space and improves the efficiency and stability of forward-only optimization.
(2) \textbf{Source-Anchored Objective Smoothing (SAOS).}
To address the severe fluctuation of entropy-driven objectives under single-sample streaming adaptation, SAOS stabilizes the forward-perturbation feedback of ZOO.
It uses source-domain feature statistics as stable anchors and aggregates target-domain branch feature statistics with a lightweight streaming queue, providing a smoother representation-level constraint for the optimization objective.
As a result, ZOO directions can maintain a more stable update trend under source-statistical consistency and naturally generalize to heterogeneous multimodal architectures.
RSLS and SAOS operate synergistically: RSLS narrows and cleans the ZOO search space at the sample and parameter-layer levels, while SAOS smooths the perturbation feedback at the objective level.
Together, they enable stable and effective online unsupervised adaptation under resource-constrained, single-sample streaming, and continuously shifting multimodal edge deployment. Our contributions are summarized as follows:

\begin{itemize}
    \item We are the first to propose BFCA, a backpropagation-free framework for edge-side multimodal continual test-time adaptation. Built on zeroth-order optimization, BFCA performs online adaptation using only forward passes under single-sample streaming deployment, and naturally supports multimodal architectures with heterogeneous modality branches and fusion modules.
    \item We provide a diagnostic analysis identifying two core challenges of applying ZOO to multimodal CTTA: inefficient search caused by dual unreliability in sample and parameter spaces, and unstable direction estimation caused by noisy entropy-driven objectives. To address them, we introduce RSLS and SAOS, which reduce search noise and optimization dimensionality while providing stable, low-variance forward-only learning signals for ZOO.
    \item We conduct comprehensive experiments with CAV-MAE on Kinetics50-C and VGGSound-C multimodal corruption benchmarks, and further evaluate BFCA on quantized backbones to validate its edge-side deployment value. Results show that BFCA significantly improves adaptation stability and overall performance while preserving backpropagation-free and low-resource adaptation.
\end{itemize}
