\paragraph{Multimodal Test-Time Adaptation.}
Multimodal test-time adaptation aims to mitigate performance degradation caused by target-domain shifts in multimodal models.
Compared with single-modal TTA and CTTA methods that mainly rely on entropy minimization, sample filtering, or robust online updating \citep{wang2021tent,niu2022eata,niu2023sar,wang2022continualtta}, multimodal TTA must additionally handle changing modality reliability under modality-specific corruptions.
Recent methods therefore diagnose shifted or unreliable modalities and mitigate their influence through reliability modeling, modality selection, asymmetric adaptation, or supervision correction \citep{guo2025smoothing,yang2024read,chen2025tsa,he2026dasp}.
Despite their effectiveness, these methods typically still update model parameters through backpropagation, requiring activation storage and gradient computation.
This makes them difficult to deploy directly in resource-constrained, forward-only, single-sample streaming edge scenarios.
Different from these BP-based multimodal adaptation methods, this work studies how to achieve stable multimodal CTTA without backpropagation.

\paragraph{Backpropagation-Free Test-Time Adaptation.}
To reduce the memory and computational costs of backpropagation, BP-free TTA methods perform adaptation with lightweight statistics or forward computations.
Prediction-time BatchNorm adaptation updates normalization statistics using target data \citep{nado2020predictiontime}, T3A adjusts classifier prototypes at test time \citep{iwasawa2021t3a}, LAME corrects model outputs without updating parameters \citep{boudiaf2022lame}, and FOA optimizes lightweight prompts through derivative-free search \citep{niu2024forwardonly}.
These methods improve deployability in resource-constrained settings, but their adaptation capacity is usually concentrated on low-dimensional components such as statistics, prototypes, outputs, or prompts.
As a result, they are more suitable for lightweight output correction or limited parameter adjustment, and are less able to absorb persistent shifts inside modality-specific branch representations.
For multimodal CTTA, where shifts can propagate through different modality branches and alter the inputs to fusion modules, a more flexible forward-only update mechanism is needed.

\paragraph{Zeroth-Order Optimization for Forward-Only Adaptation.}
Zeroth-order optimization estimates update directions with forward perturbations and finite-difference feedback, eliminating the need for backpropagation and offering favorable memory efficiency and architectural generality.
Recent forward-only TTA work shows that such derivative-free learning can support online unsupervised adaptation \citep{niu2024forwardonly}.
However, existing ZOO-based TTA is mainly studied in single-modal settings.
Directly extending it to multimodal CTTA is non-trivial because unreliable samples can corrupt perturbation feedback, high-dimensional branch parameters make search inefficient, and fluctuating entropy objectives can induce high-variance direction estimates.
This work extends ZOO to edge-side multimodal CTTA by using RSLS to stabilize the search space and SAOS to smooth the optimization objective.
